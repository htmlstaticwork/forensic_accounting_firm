Our Approach

A rigorous, systematic method to uncovering the truth.

1

Initial Consultation

We begin with a strictly confidential meeting to understand the nuances of your suspicion or dispute, identifying immediately what resources and specialized skills are required.

2

Case Assessment

A preliminary review of available financial documentation. This allows us to formulate a highly targeted investigation plan that limits unnecessary scope and expense.

3

Evidence Collection

Securing electronic data, preserving physical documents, and conducting necessary interviews under attorney-client privilege when engaged through counsel.

4

Analysis & Reporting

Data is reconstructed, traced, and analyzed by our CPAs and CFEs. The conclusion is a powerful, easily understood narrative backed entirely by documented evidence.

5

Court Support

Our work culminates in compelling expert testimony. We simplify complex accounting irregularities for judges and juries to secure decisions in your favor.

What To Expect During The Investigation

Transparency and clarity from day one.

Discretion

Covert data extraction mechanisms ensuring targets remain unaware until the evidence is fully secured.

Communication

Weekly secured status reports via our encrypted Client Portal detailing investigative progress and immediate findings.

Objectivity

An unbiased interpretation of the facts. We pursue truth, guaranteeing our testimony remains unimpeachable.

Military-Grade Data Security

Financial forensics involves highly sensitive proprietary information. We employ end-to-end 256-bit encryption for all data transfers, air-gapped storage for critical digital forensics, and strictly controlled chain-of-custody protocols.

Our secure Client Vault allows counsel and authorized personnel to share files without risking interception.

Process FAQs

How long does a typical investigation take?

Depending on complexity and data availability, initial assessments take 1-2 weeks, while full litigation engagements may last several months.

Do you work directly with my attorney?

Yes, working under the direction of counsel is standard practice to maintain attorney-client privilege over our work product.
